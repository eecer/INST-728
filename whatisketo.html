What is Keto in a nutshell?

The ketogenic diet is a high-fat, some protein and low-carb diet which typically limits carbs to 20â€“50 grams per day. Generally, the suggested ratios are 70-80% fat from total daily calories, 5-10% carbohydrate, and 10-20% protein. This diet is used in medicine to treat epilepsy in children. While un-learning that the fat is not bad for us is challenging, it is really easy to practice!

What does Keto mean?

This diet forces the body to burn fat rather than carbs and during this process, the liver converts fat into fatty acids and ketone bodies. An elevated level of ketone bodies in the blood puts the body in a state called ketosis.
